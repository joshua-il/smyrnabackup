GENERAL BACKGROUND OF THE LAND AND IT’S PEOPLE

The people of Manipur are grouped into three main ethnic communities –the Meiteis who are mostly Hindus, inhabiting the valley, and 29 major tribes in the hills dividing into two main ethno-denominations, namely Nagas and Kukis-Chins tribes, who are mostly Christian. Under the Meitei, Bamon and Meitei Pangals (Muslim) are also included. All of them speak Meitei-lon or otherwise known as Manipuri to the outsiders. At present several people from the hills have also migrated and settled in the valley.
The Naga group consists of Zeliangrong (composed of three related tribes, namely, Rongmei or Kabui, and Liangmei and Zemei or Kacha Nagas), Tangkhul, Mao, Maram, Maring, Paomei, and Tarao and so on. The Chin-Kuki group consists of Gangte, Hmar, Mizo, Paite, Thadou, Vaiphei, Simte, Tedim-Chin, and Zou and so on. It should be noted that all the different ethnic groups are of the same Mongoloid group, and have very close similarities in their culture and traditional habits. The legend that all the tribes including Meiteis claim that they originated somewhere in the North from a cave. The difference came only in later parts of the history after Meiteis were converted to Vaishnavism and the hill inhabitants became Christian.

The very significant and important contribution of the Nations Reach to the theological Students was conducting “The First Theological Graduating Students Meet ‘05” for the outgoing students. This may not be the first theological graduating students meet in the town, but the reason why it is the first is that, before each college or denomination conduct the graduating service by themselves. And this was the first time ever happened that interdenominational graduating service that was conducted in the town, that is the reason it is known as “The First Theological Graduating Students Meet ’05.” The purpose of the meeting was to revitalize the call and commitments for the students as they enter into the ministry. Around 150 (Hundred fifty) graduating students were given a gift, which was an encouraging for them as they enter the ministry.
